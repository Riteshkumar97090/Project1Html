Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. His full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. He belonged to a middle-class family. His father Karamchand Gandhi was the Diwan of a small princely state of Kathiawar during the British Raj. Gandhiji's mother's name was Putlibai The biography of Mahatma Gandhi is full of struggles. He led many movements and brought freedom to the country by following the principle of non-violence.

Some special things related to the life of Mahatma Gandhi:

  • Gandhiji led many movements like Non-cooperation Movement, Civil Disobedience Movement, Quit India Movement
  • Gandhiji had started the Dandi march.
  • Gandhiji had launched a movement against the landlords
  • Gandhi ji did works like cleanliness, building schools, hospitals among the villagers
  • Gandhiji is considered a symbol of non-violence and truth
  • Gandhi ji was assassinated on January 30, 1948 in Delhi

Mahatma Gandhi led many movements against British colonial rule, including:

  • Champaran Movement: In 1917, Gandhi led this movement in Bihar
  • Kheda Movement: In 1918, Gandhi led this movement in Gujarat

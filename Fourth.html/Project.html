India

India became independent from British rule on August 15, 1947 . This day is celebrated as Independence Day. Many freedom fighters including Mahatma Gandhi, Bhagat Singh, Mangal Pandey, Lala Lajpat Rai, Chandrashekhar Azad, Ramprasad Bismil, Subhash Chandra Bose played an important role in liberating India.
British Prime Minister Clement Attlee also had an important contribution in getting independence for India. Attlee defeated the then Prime Minister Winston Churchill in the general election of July 1945. Churchill was against India's independence, but after Attlee became the Prime Minister, the path to India's independence became clear.
After India gained independence, its Constitution came into force on 26 January 1950. According to this, India was declared a democratic, sovereign, and republic country.

India is a diverse country with many different cultures, including:

  • Hinduism: The most widely practiced religion in India, with about 80% of the population identifying as Hindu. Hinduism is based on the concepts of karma and dharma, and the goal of achieving moksha.
  • Islam: The second most widely practiced religion in India, with about 14% of the population identifying as Muslim.
  • Other religions: Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, Jainism, Zoroastrianism, and the Bahá'í Faith are also practiced in India.

Languages

  • India has 22 official languages, including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam. People also speak a variety of local languages depending on where they live.

Dance

  • Classical and folk dances in India are based on stories that are passed down orally from generation to generation. Classical dances are performed according to the rules and guidelines of the Natyashastra.
